Frequently Asked Questions

Why?
Have you ever had to write a program where you have to extract certain bits from an integer, or something along those lines, and spent the greater portion of your time figuring out what exact bitmask to use? As programs get more complex, you want to spend your time thinking about the logic of the program, not the constants. Synthesizers arose to aid programmers in writing programs quickly, by taking away the mental burden of computing the exact values of specific constants. The idea is that, eventually, you just write code and leave certain portions blank that you can't figure out, and the computer will fill in the blanks for you.

Is this some artificial intelligence nonsense? Will it always work?
Synthesizers do not work with some artificial intelligence engine at its back. Instead, the constants computed by the synthesizer are decided with a purely mathematical understanding of the program. It is entireley deterministic, and will always give you the right constant if there is one to be found!

What is the future for synthesizers?
Predicting the future of any technology is a difficult task. However, the loose goal of synthesizers is to become more broad in their synthesizing capability. That is, better synthesizers in the future will hopefully be able to generate larger portions of the code for the user!
